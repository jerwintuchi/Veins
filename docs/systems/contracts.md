## Purpose

How an expedition is generated. A contract is a roll across orthogonal axes that
multiply, which is why replayability comes from systems, not from handcrafted encounters.

## Design Philosophy

### The axes multiply

A contract is drawn server-side from the expedition seed across six axes:

| Axis | Examples |
|------|----------|
| **Target** | which Incarnate, plus its hidden trait roll and mutation stack ([incarnates.md](incarnates.md)) |
| **Site** | the biome: route topology + environmental modifiers |
| **Condition** | weather, time, sacred-decay level (changes visibility, behavior, which rites work) |
| **Primary Verb** | not always *kill*: capture alive, observe-and-survive, banish by rite, drive off |
| **Secondary Objective** | escort, rescue, retrieve, interact, defend, plus emergent complications |
| **Clause** | bring it back intact, no fire on consecrated ground, the reliquary must not break |

A handful of values per axis already yields thousands of distinct contracts before any
asset is reused. **Origin colors; the contract dictates** (TD-015): the "be careful with
the relic" pressure is a Clause, not a property of Relic-born, which is what keeps the same
Origin producing both careful and aggressive hunts.

### The board is free, the rank is the gate

Seekers pick any unlocked contract from the board at will; **Collegium Rank** gates access
to higher **tiers** (TD-012). Tier sets diagnosis depth and difficulty (TD-014): more trait
axes exposed, mutations present, the Origin itself possibly falsified. Ranking up earns
harder *readings*, never raw power.

### Intel is partial, and sometimes wrong

The contract carries the Collegium's intel, which is a lossy and occasionally mistaken
projection of the true roll, including a *claimed* Origin that may be false (TD-015). The
theory you build before deploying is a bet, which is what gives preparation teeth.

## Non-negotiable Rules

1. Intel may be wrong; the game **never guarantees the theory is correct** (gameplay.md non-negotiable 1).
2. Every **secondary objective is a lever on the central hunt** (gameplay.md non-negotiable 2), never a chore beside it.
3. **Systems over content** (vision.md non-negotiable 6): variety comes from axis combinations, not shipped encounters.
4. Tier gates **depth, not raw power** (TD-014). Acceptance stakes the **Surety** (TD-004, TD-017).

## Implementation Notes

- **Data shape:** `Contract = { incarnate: IncarnateRoll, site, condition, primaryVerb, secondaryObjective, clause, tier, intel: PartialIntel }`. Generated by a pure `generateContract(seed, tier)` server-side (I3, I5).
- **PartialIntel** is a deliberately lossy and noisy view of the true roll: it reveals some signs/Origin claim, withholds others, and may assert a wrong Origin at high tier.
- **Stipend reward** scales with tier, objectives met, and clauses honored (TD-017).
- **The board** is the set of contracts available to a Seeker, filtered by Collegium Rank. Contract generation is new code; seeded RNG and the existing room/session lifecycle are reused.
